Insights & Recommendations
Marketing Team Brief — Lead Pipeline Analysis
Jun 2024 – Feb 2025 · 401 leads
Overall performance

Across the 9-month period, the pipeline generated 401 leads with a strong overall enrollment rate of 72.8% (292 enrolled students). This is a healthy baseline, but the headline number conceals meaningful variance: enrollment rates range from below 30% in some months to above 90% in others, depending almost entirely on two variables — group connection status and month of acquisition.

The team's most efficient months were August, October, and November 2024, each achieving 85–95%+ enrollment rates with relatively few follow-up contacts required (avg. 1.2–1.5). July 2024, by contrast, generated the most no-response leads (32 of 78), dragging its enrollment rate to 52.6% despite being a high-volume month.

Key insight 1: group connection is the #1 lever

The data reveals a 37-percentage-point gap between group-connected leads (90.1% enrollment) and non-group leads (53.2%). This is the single most actionable finding in the dataset. Group-connected leads also require fewer follow-ups (avg. 1.4 vs 2.9), meaning they convert faster and consume less team capacity.

The recommendation is clear: prioritize outreach through student groups, clubs, international student offices, and university cohorts over cold individual outreach. The 188 non-group leads that came through represent a significant efficiency loss — many eventually enrolled, but at a much higher effort cost.

Key insight 2: no-response leads need a different approach

The 70 no-response leads averaging 5.2 follow-up attempts signal a broken loop rather than passive disinterest. Continuing to contact these leads with the same cadence is unlikely to change outcomes. A dedicated re-engagement campaign — different channel, different message, or a limited-time offer — could realistically recover 25–35 additional enrollments, representing meaningful revenue upside.

Key insight 3: seasonality is unmanaged

Lead volume peaks sharply in July–September 2024 (258 of 401 leads, or 64%) then falls off a cliff. January and February bring in just 23 combined leads. This mirrors the academic enrollment calendar, but it also represents a structural risk — the business has no off-season pipeline strategy.

OPT students (post-graduation work authorization) are the one segment with year-round enrollment potential. At a 75.9% overall enrollment rate, and with OPT visa timelines spread across all 12 months, a dedicated OPT campaign running November through March could meaningfully smooth the pipeline.

Key insight 4: product mix reflects price sensitivity

Prime 500 dominates enrolled policies (55%), but Prime 100 holds 26% and is particularly prevalent among undergraduate students, who also show the lowest enrollment rate (68.6%). This suggests price is a barrier for undergrads. Targeted cost-forward messaging — emphasizing affordability, payment flexibility, or value comparison with school-sponsored plans — could close the gap.

Recommended actions
  • Shift acquisition budget toward group-connected channels (clubs, offices, cohorts) to maximize enrollment rate per lead.
  • Launch a re-engagement campaign for the 70 no-response leads using a new channel or incentive — do not continue the same email cadence.
  • Build an off-season OPT pipeline running Nov–Mar to reduce Q1 volume cliff and smooth revenue.
  • Test price-sensitive messaging for undergraduate leads, highlighting Prime 100 as an accessible entry point.
  • Investigate July's high no-response rate — was outreach timing, messaging, or lead source the cause?
