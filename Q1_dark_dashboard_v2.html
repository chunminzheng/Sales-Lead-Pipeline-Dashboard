Executive summary
Marketing Team Brief — Lead Pipeline Analysis
Jun 2024 – Feb 2025 · 401 leads · Prepared by Chunmin
Overall performance

The pipeline generated 401 leads over 9 months with a strong overall enrollment rate of 72.8% (292 students). This is a healthy baseline, but the headline number masks wide variance — enrollment rates range from below 30% in some months to above 90% in others, driven almost entirely by two variables: group connection status and acquisition timing.

The team's most efficient months were August, October, and November 2024, achieving 85–95%+ enrollment with minimal follow-up effort (avg. 1.2–1.5 contacts). July 2024 was the weakest — 32 of 78 leads went cold, dragging enrollment to 52.6% despite being a high-volume month.

Insight 1: group connection is the #1 lever

Group-connected leads enroll at 90.1% vs just 53.2% for unconnected leads — a 37-point gap. This is the single most actionable finding in the dataset. Group-connected leads also convert faster, requiring fewer follow-ups (avg. 1.4 vs 2.9), meaning they generate more enrollments per unit of team effort.

The implication is clear: shift acquisition toward student clubs, international student offices, cohorts, and partner groups rather than cold individual outreach. The 188 non-group leads represent a significant efficiency loss — many enrolled, but at a much higher effort cost.

Insight 2: no-response leads need a new approach

70 leads are stuck in no-response status despite averaging 5.2 follow-up attempts each. Continuing the same outreach cadence is unlikely to change outcomes. A re-engagement campaign using a different channel, a fresh message angle, or a time-limited offer could realistically recover 25–35 additional enrollments — representing meaningful upside with zero new lead acquisition cost.

Insight 3: seasonality is unmanaged

Lead volume peaks sharply in July–September 2024 (258 of 401 leads, 64% of the full-year pipeline) then collapses — January and February bring in just 23 leads combined. This mirrors the academic enrollment calendar but represents a structural risk: the business has no meaningful off-season strategy.

OPT students are the one segment with year-round enrollment potential. At a 75.9% overall enrollment rate and with OPT visa timelines distributed across all 12 months, a dedicated OPT outreach campaign running November through March could meaningfully smooth the Q1 volume cliff.

Insight 4: price sensitivity among undergraduates

Prime 500 dominates enrolled policies at 55%, but Prime 100 holds 26% and is disproportionately popular among undergraduates — who also show the lowest enrollment rate of any segment (68.6%). This suggests price is a conversion barrier for undergrads. Targeted messaging that leads with affordability or directly compares Prime 100 to the school-sponsored plan could close this gap.

Recommended actions
  • Prioritize group channels. Shift acquisition budget toward student clubs, cohort programs, and international offices to exploit the 37-point enrollment rate gap.
  • Re-engage the 70 stalled leads. Try a different channel or incentive — do not continue the same email cadence.
  • Build an OPT off-season pipeline. Target Nov–Mar outreach to smooth the Q1 drop-off and reduce seasonal revenue risk.
  • Lead with price for undergrads. Highlight Prime 100 as an accessible entry point; test cost-comparison messaging against school plans.
  • Investigate July's no-response spike. 32 cold leads in a single month warrants a root-cause review — was it timing, lead source, or messaging?
